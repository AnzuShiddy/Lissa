Privacy

Plain language, no legal filler. This describes the hosted app at lissa-02zl.onrender.com. If you're running Lissa yourself from the source on GitHub — the terminal app, or your own deployment — you control that instance's data entirely; none of it touches this one.

What she remembers about you

Long-term memory (your name, preferences, things you've told her) is stored only in your browser's local storage — never on our server. It's sent up with each request just to personalize your own session, and it updates automatically as you chat. Use "Forget me" in the 🧠 panel to erase it instantly; nothing needs to be requested from us, because we never had a copy.

What's stored on the server

Nothing persists. Each conversation lives in server memory only, and is automatically discarded after about 4 hours of inactivity — there's no database. A single cookie (sid) identifies your session so your messages land in the right conversation; it carries no personal information and isn't used for tracking or advertising.

We also keep lightweight usage counters (timestamps and counts, not message content) to enforce fair-use rate limits, since the app runs on one shared API key. These reset daily and aren't tied to anything identifying beyond your anonymous session.

Voice and photos

Recording only happens when you start it (tapping the mic, or turning on hands-free mode). Audio is sent off for transcription and speech synthesis and is not saved afterward. Photos you attach are sent for that one exchange and aren't written to disk — though, like the rest of a conversation, they remain part of the in-memory session until it expires.

Where your messages go

Two outside services process what you send, so Lissa can reply:

  • Google's Gemini API — your text, photos, and voice recordings, to generate her replies and understand what you've sent. See Gemini API Terms and Google's Privacy Policy.
  • Microsoft's Edge neural voice service — the text of her replies, to synthesize her spoken voice. See Microsoft's Privacy Statement.

We don't control how these providers handle data on their end — their own policies apply to that processing, not ours.

The app is hosted on Render, which, like any host, may keep its own standard infrastructure logs (request timing, IP address) under its privacy policy — separate from anything this app itself stores.

Who this is for

Lissa is a flirtatious companion persona, written for adults. There's no age verification — there just isn't a way for a project like this to check — so we're asking directly: if you're a minor, please don't use it.

Your choices

  • "Forget me" (🧠 panel) wipes your local memory instantly.
  • "Save chat" downloads your conversation as a text file, if you want a copy before it expires.
  • Closing the tab or clearing cookies ends your session; there's no account to delete, because none exists.
